- given that an electric wave propagates along the $\hat{z}$ direction
$$
E(z,t)=\vec{E_{0}}e^{i(kz-\omega t)}=E_{x} \hat{x}=E_{y} \hat{y}
$$
- such that
$$
E_{x}=E_{0,x}e^{i(kz-\omega t+\delta_{x})} 
$$
$$
E_{y}=E_{0,y}e^{i(kz-\omega t+ \delta y)}
$$
- where $\delta_{x}, \delta_{y}$ represent the phase different between the two polarizations. 
- Linear polarization is achieved when either $E_{x}=0,E_{y}=0$, **OR** if $\delta=\delta_{x}-\delta_{y}=0,\delta=\pi$

- Circular polarization is achieved when $E_{0,x}=E_{0,y}$ **AND** $\delta=\pm \frac{\pi}{2}$
